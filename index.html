Student Groups


  • Groups of students can be created to track information that relates to that group from attendance, degrees, to health and wellness.
  • Both teachers and administrators can create groups.
  • One teacher created a group for struggling readers. He implemented a new strategy and then tracked that group over time to see if the strategy positively affected those students.
  • Once you created a group, you can easily view the students’ data, compare the data with other groups, and even share the information with other teachers or admins.

Creating a Student Group - Method One


  • Select the tools drop-down menu and select manage groups. We are going to create a new group. Give it a name, a date range, a description, decide whether it is public or private, choose a type, and now we are going to add members.
  • You can do this manually and add them singly or multiple students at the same time. Start typing in the search field to find an individual student. When you find them, select them from the list and you will see their names appeared below. If you want to add multiple students at the same time, you can enter a list of students ids. Once you selected all the members for your group, select save at the bottom and you will see our new group has been added to the group management list.

Creating a Student Group - Method Two


  • Let’s make a small group of students who are all in the AP calculus.
  • So I am going up to Academics, choose AP, and I am going to left-click on my graph to show AP calculus students. I am also going to use the filter data button to a specific school and a specific grade.
  • If I’ve already created that group, I could use the drop-down menu. But I can also create a new group here which I will do.
